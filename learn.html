What is OpenBox?

OpenBox Programs tackles resource and education inequity at its roots by presenting a free, public curriculum that builds core soft skills and high-level computational thinking in middle school students.

These resources are self-paced and available to anyone, regardless of your background!

NOTE: The curriculum is still in development! Stay tuned for additional information.

Get one-on-one mentorship.

Select students will receive direct guidance from mentors, culminating in the development of a high-level project. This sets students up for future, accelerated learning in CS and develops the skills needed to explore advanced topics independently, increasing representation in the computer science field. Students from underprivileged backgrounds or communities that are traditionally underrepresented in STEM are encouraged to apply.

Students in grades 6-8 are encouraged to apply. If you are apply during the summer, please use the grade you will be going to.

Become a Mentor

Inspire the next generation of mathematics and computer science students by becoming a mentor! Spend 1-2 hours a week working with motivated middle school students. Answer questions about the curriculum, give insight into your high school or college experience, and work with your students to develop a high level research project!

High school and college students are encouraged to apply! While a research or project development is not necessary, it is highly encouraged. Volunteer hours may be earned at your request.
